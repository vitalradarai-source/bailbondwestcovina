LASD West Covina Station: What Happens After an Arrest in West Covina

West Covina is an incorporated city in the San Gabriel Valley area of Los Angeles County. Most arrests in West Covina are handled by the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD) under contract. The primary booking facility for West Covina arrests is LASD West Covina Station, located at 1121 S. Sunset Ave, West Covina, CA 91790, phone (626) 960-1955.

LASD West Covina Station is a full-service station jail that handles booking and short-term detention for the area. For serious felony cases, court-ordered holds, or defendants who exceed station capacity, LASD transfers inmates to central facilities:

  • Men's Central Jail (MCJ) — 441 Bauchet St, Los Angeles, CA 90012. The primary felony intake facility for LA County, one of the largest in the United States.
  • Twin Towers Correctional Facility (TTCF) — 450 Bauchet St, Los Angeles, CA 90012. Adjacent to MCJ; handles pre-trial detainees including those requiring medical or mental health services.

West Covina arraignments are heard at the West Covina Courthouse, 1427 W. Covina Pkwy, West Covina, CA 91790, a branch of the Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles. Arraignment must occur within 48 hours of arrest under California Penal Code § 825 (excluding Sundays and court holidays).

To check inmate status, the LASD operates an online inmate locator at shq.lasd.org. Records typically appear 2 to 6 hours after booking begins.

The Booking Process: Step-by-Step Timeline at LASD West Covina Station

Every arrest in West Covina follows the same sequence at 1121 S. Sunset Ave. Here is what happens at each step:

Step 1
Arrest and Transport
The LASD deputy or contracting officer places the defendant in custody and transports them to LASD West Covina Station. The arresting officer's report details the charges and the factual basis for the arrest. This document drives the bail determination.
Step 2
Property Inventory
All personal effects — phone, wallet, keys, jewelry — are inventoried, bagged, and logged. A property receipt is issued. The phone being confiscated is why families typically can't reach the person for several hours after an arrest.
Step 3
Live Scan Fingerprinting
Electronic fingerprints are submitted to the California DOJ and FBI to confirm identity and run a warrant check. Outstanding warrants from other California counties or other states can result in a hold that delays release even after bail is posted.
Step 4
Booking Photo
Standard booking photographs taken for the official LASD record. Accessible through the LASD inmate locator once the record is fully processed.
Step 5
Medical Screening
California law mandates a health screening at booking. This covers medications, injury assessment, mental health needs, and communicable disease screening. Defendants with serious medical conditions spend more time in this phase.
Step 6
Bail Set Per LA County Schedule
Bail is set according to the annual LA County Felony and Misdemeanor Bail Schedule published by the Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles. The schedule amount applies automatically at booking for most charges. A judge at the West Covina Courthouse can modify bail at arraignment.
Step 7
Housing or Transfer Decision
Misdemeanor defendants are typically held at West Covina Station until bail is posted or released. Felony defendants face potential transfer to MCJ or Twin Towers. We track these transfers so you never have to search for a new facility location on your own.

For misdemeanor charges, expect the process at LASD West Covina Station to take 4 to 8 hours. For felonies with potential transfer to downtown LA facilities, the total window extends to 12 to 24 hours from time of arrest to bail eligibility.

LA County Bail Schedule: What Common Charges Cost in West Covina

West Covina is in Los Angeles County, meaning the Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles bail schedule applies. Here are representative bail amounts for charges commonly processed through West Covina Station:

Charge Code Section Bail Amount
DUI — first offense, no injury VC 23152 $5,000
DUI with bodily injury VC 23153 $100,000
Assault with deadly weapon PC 245(a)(1) $30,000
Robbery (second degree) PC 211 $75,000
Residential burglary PC 459 (1st degree) $50,000
Drug possession (controlled substance) HS 11350 $10,000
Possession for sale HS 11351 $30,000
Grand theft auto VC 10851 $20,000

These amounts are set at booking by LASD staff. At arraignment at the West Covina Courthouse, a judge can raise, reduce, or eliminate bail. Posting bail before arraignment preserves the scheduled amount. This is especially important on weekends and days preceding court holidays when arraignment windows are compressed.

How a Bail Bond Works in West Covina (10% Premium, Co-Signer)

When someone in West Covina is arrested and bail is set at $30,000, producing $30,000 in cash is not feasible for most families — particularly in the early morning hours when most bail calls come in. A bail bond addresses this directly:

  • 10% premium under CA law. California Insurance Code § 1800.4 sets the standard bail bond premium at 10% of the total bail. For $30,000 bail, the premium is $3,000 — the fee paid to the bondsman for guaranteeing the full amount. The premium is non-refundable regardless of how the case resolves.
  • Bond delivered to LASD. Once signed, Angels Bail Bonds delivers the bond to LASD West Covina Station (or to MCJ/Twin Towers if the defendant has been transferred) authorizing the release order.
  • Co-signer required. A co-signer — usually a family member or close friend — signs the agreement and accepts responsibility for the defendant's court appearances. Failure to appear can expose the co-signer to liability for the full bail amount.
  • Payment plans available. Zero-down and structured payment plans are available for qualifying clients. Most families cannot produce $3,000 immediately — we work with you.
  • Fully remote process. The entire agreement can be completed by phone or email. You don't need to come to an office or drive downtown.
